FT Certification

Organization Certification Process

  • Fair Trade Forum - India (FTF-I) certifies organizations as Fair Trade Organization. If an organization wants to be certified (in India) they need to be a member of FTF-I.
  • The organization needs to fill up a detailed application form, which after the primary assessment goes to the Executive Director of FTF-I for clearance from the Executive Council.
  • After receiving the application form, FTF-I will do an Entry Audit of the organization to screen the potential and eligibility for certification. Based upon the results of the entry audit the organization will qualify or be re-audited.
  • All products are eligible to have the organizations Fair Trade label as per all Fair Trade adherences (10 standards), under the organization Fair Trade certification.

General conditions applicable for Membership.

  • This being a membership-based organization, the membership is open to all eligible NGOs, Trusts, Cooperatives, producers and marketing organizations, subscribing to the objects and rules of the Forum. Individuals can seek associate membership but cannot become a full-fledged member of the Forum.
  • The rights of the membership admission rests with the Executive Council of the Forum.
  • Applicants, have to apply on the prescribed form duly proposed and seconded by at least two existing member organizations of the Forum. No recommendation is required, if the applicant is already a member of World Fair Trade Organization (WFTO) or World Fair Trade Organization-Asia (WFTO-Asia).
  • All organizations applying for the membership should have minimum three years existence in their respective area of working.
  • The organizations have to make sustained efforts and also facilitate in initiating, expanding, strengthening and maintaining Fair Trade standards in their trading/income generating activities.

Fees

  • The membership application processing fee Rs.500/- (non-refundable) by way of demand draft only drawn in favour of ‘FAIR TRADE FORUM – INDIA’ payable at New Delhi, shall be send along with the filled-in Application Form.
  • The present annual fee is Rs. 2,500/- only for member organizations, whose annual turnover is Rs. 10 lakhs and above, and Rs.1000/- if the annual turnover is less than 10 lakhs. The Annual fee could be sent after approval of the membership application.

Entry Audit

  • As per the procedures of FTF-I membership application process, all new applicant organizations need to undergo an Entry Audit for a possible recommendation for Fair Trade membership.
  • The purpose of the Entry Audit is to assess the level of organization strategies and policies on adherence of Fair Trade principles and the commitment towards Fair Trade and Producer Groups. The duration of the Entry Audit shall be of 3 days including one day orientation session.
  • The orientation session will help you to understand Fair Trade in detail and how the implementation of Fair Trade principles can contribute to develop a long-term sustainable development. The presence of the Chief Functionary, one or more key person(s) among the Board Members and all important staff are essential during the orientation session.
  • The Fee for the Entry Audit is Rs. 6,000/- per day. (Apart from the fee, Travel, Accommodation, Food & Refreshment expenses need to be reimbursed directly to the Auditor on submissions of bills/ vouchers).
  • After completion of the Orientation session, the Organization have the liberty to decide, whether they go ahead with the Entry Audit or they need more time, for preparation and conduct the Entry Audit at a later stage, the organization may also get suggestions from the Entry Auditor.

Product Certification Process

How to get the Shop for Change Mark on your product

Getting the Shop for Change Mark on your product is straight forward and hassle free. We are here to take you through the process and support you on an ongoing basis.


Step-by-step guide to becoming a Shop for Change Partner Brand

  • Step 1 -
    Talk with the Shop for Change Business Development Team and learn more about the work they do and how you can partner to help farmers, sell your products and talk about your achievements to your customers and stakeholders.
  • Step 2 -
    Work with Shop for Change team to devise a supply chain strategy to seamlessly integrate Shop for Change certified commodities into your products.
  • Step 3 -
    TGet to know the Shop for Change standards for buyers and sign a license agreement with Shop for Change Fair Trade for use of the Shop for Change Mark on your products.
  • Step 4 -
    Tag your products with the Shop for Change Mark and work with us to promote your product and communicate effectively about fair trade.

For more details on Product Certification and Shop for Change standards, Please visit http://shopforchange.in
